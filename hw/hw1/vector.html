Now that we know the basics of what a Vector image is, lets go into more detail.

Since Vector graphics consist only of mathematical formulas, Vector images retain their appearance regardless of size.

Vector graphics can be scaled infinitely, the mathematical equations that form the foundations for Vectors recalculate when theyâ€™re resized. Thus maintaining crisp, sharp edges.

Vector images are made of shapes, and each shape has its own color.

Vectors should not be used for digital paintings or photo editing.

With the exception of the SVG format, Vectors must be rasterized before they can be used on the web.


Usages:

  • High definition logos
  • Drawings and animations
  • Computer fonts

Drawbacks:

  • Less detailed
  • Solid colors only
  • Require special programs to create
